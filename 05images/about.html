Workouts

There are a few ways you can structure a workout. I will be going over the most effective way to hit all major muscle groups. Cardio is also an important part of your program, so make sure to add it in to the end.

The three components of a good workout program are as follows:

Compound Lifts
Compound Lifts- This type of lifting will target multiple muscle groups at once and usually (but not always) involves multiple joints bending to perform the lift.
Accessory Lifts
These target just one muscle group at a time.
Cardio
Does not usually involve weight. The goal is to improve your cardiovascular health by elevating your heart rate.

All of these components are needed in a well rounded program. Below I have made two seperate lists. One of compound lifts, and one of accessory lifts. To build your workout pick anywhere from 1 to 3 compound lifts per workout, along with 2 to 4 accessory lifts per workout. Remember to take your current fitness and activity level into account. If you do too much too soon your chances of injury will increase.

Compound Lifts

  • Back Squat
  • Dead Lift
  • Bench Press
  • Pull up
  • Overhead Press
  • Row
  • Barbell Lunges

Accessory Lifts

  • Bicep curls
  • Hammer Curls
  • Overhead Tricep Extension
  • Cable Tricep Extension
  • Lateral Raises
  • Front Raises
  • Face Pulls
  • Machine Chest Fly
  • Cable Row
  • Seated Row
  • Lat Pulldown
  • Dumbell Row
  • Leg Extensions
  • Leg Curls
  • Leg Press
  • Good Mornings
  • Calf Raises

Cardio

  • Walking
  • Running/jogging
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Rowing
  • Dancing
